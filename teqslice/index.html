Transparency mode finds disconnected opaque regions using the alpha channel. Flat color mode is for sheets that were exported without real transparency (a solid background color, or a checkerboard baked into the pixels as flat colors) — it samples the sheet's dominant background color(s) automatically and treats close matches as empty space instead. We pick a starting mode based on this image, but switch manually if it guesses wrong. Objects closer than the merge distance are treated as one. Results are added as ordinary, editable slices — nothing is committed until you close this dialog, and you can merge, resize, or delete any proposed slice afterward with the normal slicing tools.

Remove background

Click (Wand) or drag a shape (Lasso) to select. Shift adds to the selection, Alt subtracts. Scroll to zoom, right-click-drag to pan, double-click to reset. Tinted area is what gets removed.

Wand selects a connected region by color similarity (Tolerance controls how loose the match is). Lasso selects any shape you draw, regardless of color. Combine freely — Auto edges reproduces the old one-click flat-background detection as a starting selection, Invert flips kept vs. removed, and Feather softens the cutout edge.
